CLOSE and SCHEDULE follow-up visits

Clinicians need a method for sensitively closing the interview, especially after a traumatic history has been revealed

It is important to create a relationship and continuous dialogue with the patient

Patients greatly value the PCP’s assurance that they will take up again the patient’s trauma story during the next visit

Schedule follow-up visits and add the diagnosis to the patient’s record (“problem list”)

“Thank you for telling me about these upsetting events. You have helped me to understand your situation better”

“How would you like me to help you?” The answers of some patients may be very specific; others may have no idea what help they may need.

Make a plan with the patient that includes follow-up visits. Just having an additional conversation with the patient at a later date can do wonders for their mental strength.
