REINFORCE/TEACH positive coping behaviors

Build on the patient’s self-healing response

You can help a patient by simply reinforcing positive behavior and teaching coping techniques

Patients usually hold their PCPs in very high regard. PCPs must take advantage of this positive regard to validate whatever coping strategies are already being used

Recommend coping strategies beginning with self-care

“I want you to keep up the good work! It is good for you and will help you cope.”

“I want you to build physical, spiritual, AND mental strength.”

Ask the patient the 4 following self-healing questions:

  1. What traumatic events have happened?
  2. How are your body and mind repairing the injuries sustained from those events?
  3. What have you done in your daily life to help yourself recover?
  4. What justice do you require from society to support your personal healing?
- From Healing Invisible Wounds, p. 243
